Senior Design: Motion Detector Board

This board tracks motion with a 9DOF IMU and transmits the processed data via BLE. This design uses an ESP32-C3-Mini-N4 as the microcontroller, an ICM-20948 as the IMU, and two TLV62569DBVT switching regulators.

I wanted to take measurements of a frisbee's motion when thrown. Specifically motion such as the initial RPM, anlge of launch, stability, etc. This can all be done with the on board IMU.

The IMU has 9 degrees of freedom, a 3 axis accelerometer, gyrometer, and magnetometer each measuring in the x, y, and z direction. This IMU is important due to its small size and processing power with an on chip data processor. This is a prototype that will be reduced in size for the final product.

Arduino Voltage Measurement Shield

This is a custom voltage measurement shield I made for the arduino. This design incorperates four different circuits: a DC-DC voltage doubler, a voltage regulator with an LM317 variable linear regulator, an LM555 timer, and a voltage divider.

I was having a hard time reading the voltage of a laser displacement sensor I had that would output a signal of +-10V. The problem is that the arduino analog to digital converters (ADCs) could only read a range of 0-5V.

This shield will take in the voltage of a 9V battery, double it to a range of 16-17V, and the LM317 voltage regulator will output a percise and consistant 10V. This signal would then be added to the laser signal in order to only get possbly 0-20V. Then a simple voltage divider was used in order to get the voltage read by the on board ADCs to a comfortable 0-5V range that would be back figured to find the initial voltage on the microcontroller.
